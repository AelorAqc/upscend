For Abdullah · Upscend

I read your post for a sales consultant.

Then I put it away, and spent an hour with Upscend itself.

In your words

“The LMS that guarantees learning.” Stop measuring completion rates. Start measuring mastery.

That isn’t something a company signs up for on a Tuesday. It’s an outcome they have to be sold on. Enterprise, not self‑serve.

What you built

You gave every learner a guide.

Matar walks each employee through the parts they’d otherwise quit on. You engineered guidance into the product so no one is left confused.

But the person deciding whether to buy Upscend gets no guide at all. The hardest thing to learn here isn’t the course. It’s whether to trust Upscend.

What I first thought

“They need a better sales consultant.”

A closer, for one round of deals.
A demo team, brought in for another.
This consultant, now.
A marketing role, open right beside it.

Capable people, each holding one corner. What I don’t see is anyone who owns the whole path — from who to target, to the message, to the booked demo.

So

I didn’t apply to your post.

I looked to see whether we could own that path for you — the engine that turns strangers into qualified demos and hands them to your closers. Not another corner of it. The whole thing.

If you already have someone who owns that, ignore me. I’d rather be useless to you than pretend.

One question

Who owns turning “the LMS that guarantees learning” into booked demos today — one person, or a stack of freelancers?

Reply right here on Upwork with your answer. That’s all I need to know whether we should keep going.
